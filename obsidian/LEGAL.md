# OBSIDIAN — Legal Notice

## Authorised Use Only

OBSIDIAN is a security testing tool intended exclusively for use by security professionals
on systems they own or have received explicit written permission to test.

## What This Means

You may use OBSIDIAN to:
- Test systems and infrastructure you personally own
- Test systems where you have documented written authorisation from the system owner
- Test in isolated lab environments you control
- Conduct authorised penetration testing engagements

You may NOT use OBSIDIAN to:
- Scan systems you do not own without explicit written permission
- Conduct reconnaissance against third-party infrastructure
- Test systems where you only have verbal permission (get it in writing)
- Perform any action that constitutes unauthorised computer access

## Applicable Law

Unauthorised use of this tool may violate:
- Computer Misuse Act 1990 (United Kingdom)
- Computer Fraud and Abuse Act (United States)
- Directive 2013/40/EU on attacks against information systems (European Union)
- Equivalent legislation in your jurisdiction

Penalties can include criminal prosecution, fines, and imprisonment.

for ensuring proper authorisation before running any scan.
